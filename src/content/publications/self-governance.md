AI is prevalent in social media, powering its recommendation algorithms, face filters, and personalized advertising. Modern AI models live directly on user devices and can interact with private user data that has not been uploaded. In this work, we compare the currently deployed models for both Instagram and TikTok to their public statements about internal AI governance practices to see if these models live up to their reported standards. We found that Instagram’s model may exhibit undesirable biases in concepts that their other internal models (eg, DINOv2) may lack. We also found that TikTok’s local model is less accurate in predicting age than their internal age detection system. Both findings indicate that the user should be more involved in the AI governance process within social media.
